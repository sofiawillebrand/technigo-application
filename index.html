HTTP cookies

What are cookies?

HTTP cookies, also known as cookies, are small pieces of data with information that is registered when you browse different web pages. The cookies can be used for multiple purposes (mainly session managment, personalization and tracking), such as remembering if you are signed in to a web page, register how many times you have visited a page, save information that you enter and much more. Many times, you can choose whether to let the web page register cookies or not when you enter a page. If you don't want to register any cookies, you can use incognito mode/private browsing/privacy mode while browsing (the settings depend on your choice of browser).
